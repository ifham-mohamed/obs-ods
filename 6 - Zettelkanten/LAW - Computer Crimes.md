# LAW - Computer Crimes

#### **Overview:**

Sri Lanka's Computer Crimes Act (No. 24 of 2007) addresses illegal activities related to computers and information technology. It includes provisions for both crimes where computers are used as tools for traditional crimes and crimes that directly target computer systems.

#### **Key Points:**

1. **Scope and Applicability:**
    - Applies to crimes committed inside Sri Lanka or affecting computers located in Sri Lanka, regardless of where the crime is perpetrated.
    - Covers crimes that impact Sri Lankan entities or individuals, even if committed abroad.

1. **Types of Offenses:**
    - **Computer-Related Crimes:** Uses computers to commit traditional crimes like theft and fraud.
    - **Hacking Offenses:** Involves unauthorized access or disruption of computer systems (e.g., introducing viruses).

1. **Substantive Offenses:**
    - **Unauthorized Access:** Gaining access to a computer system without permission (Section 3).
    - **Intention to Commit Further Crimes:** Accessing a computer to commit additional offenses (Section 4).
    - **Unauthorized Modification:** Altering or damaging data or computer systems (Section 5).
    - **Economic and Security Threats:** Committing crimes affecting national security or economic interests (Section 6).
    - **Illegal Acquisition of Data:** Buying or using data obtained unlawfully (Section 7).
    - **Interception of Communications:** Illegally accessing data from communications (Section 8).
    - **Distribution of Tools for Crime:** Selling or distributing tools like passwords used to commit computer crimes (Section 9).
    - **Disclosure of Access Information:** Sharing information that enables unauthorized access (Section 10).

#### **Purpose:**

The Act aims to protect computer systems, data, and networks from unauthorized access and damage, ensuring a secure digital environment.
