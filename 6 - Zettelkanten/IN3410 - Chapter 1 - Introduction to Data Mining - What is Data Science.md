### 1. Why is Data Science needed?

Data is being generated in humongous quantities every second (from social media, sensors, mobile phones, financial transactions, health devices, etc.). Raw data by itself is of no use unless analyzed and processed.

Need: Data Science derives useful knowledge and insights from this data, leading to improved decision-making, forecasts, automation, and industry innovations.

Two examples

- Healthcare: Interpreting medical images and patient records to diagnose diseases (e.g., cancer) earlier and prescribe customized treatments.
- E-commerce: Recommending products to online shoppers on the basis of their browsing and buying history.

### 2. Where does data come from?

- Data comes from diverse sources in our daily life and businesses:
- Human-generated: Social media posts, emails, questionnaires, online reviews.
- Machine-generated: IoT device sensors, GPS location information, transaction logs, industrial equipment.
- Business processes: Sales transactions, banking transactions, supply chain data.
- Public sources: Government census, research papers, weather forecast.

### 3. What is Data Science?

Data Science is an interdisciplinary science that combines statistics, computer science, mathematics, and domain knowledge to extract meaningful patterns and insights from structured (tables, databases) and unstructured (text, images, videos) data.

It employs techniques like machine learning, artificial intelligence, big data technology, and visualization.

### 4. Who are Data Scientists?

Data Scientists are individuals who:
- Collect and tidy data.
- Use statistical models and machine learning models.
- Make conclusions to inform insights.
- Share conclusions in visualizations and reports.
- They act as a translation from raw data to business decision-making.

### 5. What are some recent applications of Data Science? (Two examples)

Autonomous Vehicles (Self-Driving Cars): Data science processes sensor, GPS, and camera data in real time to identify obstacles, traffic signs, and pedestrians to facilitate safe navigation.

Banking Fraud Detection: Machine learning routines analyze patterns of bank transactions to discover deviant patterns (e.g., credit card theft) in real-time.
