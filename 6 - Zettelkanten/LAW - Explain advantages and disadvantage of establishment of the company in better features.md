# LAW - Explain advantages and disadvantage of establishment of the company in better features

###  **Advantages and Disadvantages of Establishing a Company**

#### **Advantages:**

1. **Limited Liability:**
    
    - **Advantage:** Shareholders' liability is limited to their investment in the company, protecting personal assets from business debts and legal claims.
    - **Disadvantage:** The extent of liability protection can lead to moral hazard where directors may take excessive risks.
2. **Separate Legal Personality:**
    
    - **Advantage:** The company is a distinct legal entity capable of entering into contracts, owning assets, and being sued in its own name.
    - **Disadvantage:** This separation can sometimes lead to complex legal and regulatory compliance requirements.
3. **Perpetual Succession:**
    
    - **Advantage:** The company continues to exist regardless of changes in ownership, providing stability and continuity.
    - **Disadvantage:** Can lead to difficulties in winding up or dissolving the company if the ownership or management does not align.
4. **Access to Capital:**
    
    - **Advantage:** Especially for public companies, the ability to raise capital through issuing shares and other financial instruments is a significant advantage.
    - **Disadvantage:** Raising capital through public offerings involves substantial costs and regulatory scrutiny.
5. **Enhanced Credibility and Corporate Image:**
    
    - **Advantage:** Being a registered company can enhance credibility with clients, suppliers, and financial institutions.
    - **Disadvantage:** May require adherence to rigorous compliance and reporting standards, which can be costly.
6. **Transferability of Shares:**
    
    - **Advantage:** Shares in a public company can be easily bought and sold, providing liquidity for shareholders.
    - **Disadvantage:** In private companies, share transfers are restricted, which can limit liquidity and flexibility for shareholders.

#### **Disadvantages:**

1. **Regulatory Compliance:**
    
    - **Advantage:** Regulatory frameworks ensure transparency and accountability in business operations.
    - **Disadvantage:** Compliance with regulations can be time-consuming and costly, involving regular audits, reporting, and legal requirements.
2. **Cost of Formation and Operation:**
    
    - **Advantage:** Provides a structured approach to business with clear legal guidelines.
    - **Disadvantage:** Establishing and maintaining a company involves significant costs, including registration fees, annual reports, and legal consultations.
3. **Loss of Control:**
    
    - **Advantage:** For public companies, the ability to raise large amounts of capital can drive growth.
    - **Disadvantage:** Founders may lose control over the company due to dilution of ownership and influence from external shareholders.
4. **Complexity of Structure:**
    
    - **Advantage:** Allows for complex business structures and diversified operations.
    - **Disadvantage:** Managing a complex corporate structure can be challenging and may require sophisticated management systems and expertise.
5. **Disclosure Requirements:**
    
    - **Advantage:** Transparency in operations and financials fosters trust and accountability.
    - **Disadvantage:** Public disclosure of financial statements and operational details can expose the company to competitive disadvantages and scrutiny.

In summary, while establishing a company in Sri Lanka offers significant benefits such as limited liability, perpetual succession, and access to capital, it also comes with challenges including regulatory compliance, operational costs, and potential loss of control. The choice of business structure should align with the company's objectives, scale, and operational needs.
