### <mark style="background: #FF5582A6;">Dithering</mark>

**Dithering: The process by which we create illusions of the color that are not present actually.**

Dithering imitates the lost colors by changing or rearranging the dots in an image and peppering the pattern with similar colors.

- **Definition**: Dithering is a process by which we create illusions of colors that are not actually present.
- **Purpose**: It imitates lost colors by changing or rearranging dots in an image, creating the appearance of multiple shades.
- **Use Case**: Dithering is commonly used to improve the appearance of images where the output is limited to a specific color range (e.g., 1-bit monochrome images).

E.g.:
- a 1-bit image is monochrome and capable of only using a palette of two colors: black and white   
- Dithering can be employed to create the appearance of multiple shades by varying the distance between dots.
- You might perceive multiple shades of grey in the image, but the only colors present are black and white

### <mark style="background: #FF5582A6;">Color Correction</mark>

**Color correction is an editing technique used to adjust the color in an image to appear more realistic.**

1. **Color Correction**:
    - **Definition**: Color correction adjusts the colors in an image to appear more realistic.
    - **Function**: It converts colors in an image to other colors, allowing you to mimic specific film appearances, portray moods, or switch color spaces.
    - **Examples**: Adjusting for red-eye, achieving natural color tones, and correcting color imbalances.

Why color correct?
- Mimic appearance of a type of film
- Portray a particular mood
- Convert from one color space to another.

E.g.:
- Adjust for red eye
- Adjust color to be more natural instead of red or green tint

### <mark style="background: #FF5582A6;">Cropping</mark>

Cropping-a technique where you cut out **distracting background** or **a clutter of irrelevant objectives around an image**

- **Purpose**: Cropping involves removing distracting backgrounds or irrelevant parts from an image.
- **Benefits**: It improves an image by focusing on essential elements and eliminating unnecessary portions.
- **Method**: Use any image editing software to crop digital images effectively.

It basically divides your image into a <mark style="background: #FF5582A6;">3 x 3</mark> grid.

### <mark style="background: #FF5582A6;">Selection</mark>

- **Definition**: Selection allows you to choose specific parts of an image for editing.
- **Impact**: Altering selected areas affects only those parts, leaving the rest of the image unchanged.
- **Use Case**: Useful for targeted adjustments or enhancements.

### <mark style="background: #FF5582A6;">Scaling/Resizing/Resampling</mark>

- **Process**: Scaling resizes a digital image.
- **Effects**:
    - Scaling up (enlarging) may make pixels appear soft.
    - Scaling down (reducing) results in sharper pixels but a less visible image.
- **Considerations**: Retain perspective and maintain quality during resizing.


### <mark style="background: #FF5582A6;">Layering</mark>

- **Concept**: Layering involves stacking multiple elements (such as images, text, or effects) on top of each other.
- **Benefits**: Allows non-destructive editing, organization, and flexibility.
- **Common Tools**: Adobe Photoshop, Corel, GIMP, and other graphics software support layer-based editing.
