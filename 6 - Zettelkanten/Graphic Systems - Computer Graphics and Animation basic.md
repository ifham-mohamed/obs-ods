# Computer Graphics and Animation basic


### <mark style="background: #FF5582A6;">Graphic</mark>

*Creating or manipulating the existing images or making fully new concepts of telling the story of from it and which can be 2D or 3D,Synthetic or photographic.*

Major Areas:
- Modelling - Mathematica specification of the shapes

- Rendering - Creating shades images from 3D models

- Animation - Creating motion through images sequences

Historical of the evolve
- 1950's - First image was created by using an oscilloscope
- 1960's - Sketchpad
- 1970's - Traditional Graphic pipeline
- 1980's - Algos developed & 1985 - rendering equation 
- 1990's - Advanced algos developed & 1995 - Photon Mapping
- 2000's - Photorealistic render

Application Areas
- CAD
- Presentation
- Education & Training
- Sci-Fi Visualization
- Image Processing
- Medical field
- Entertainment
- GUI

### <mark style="background: #FF5582A6;">Raster</mark>

*Raster graphic, also known as bitmaps images, made out of using small squares know pixels*

1. Pixels - 
	1. Color value  
	2. Location
	![[Graphic Systems pixel.png]]


2. Quality of Raster Image - Pixels Per Inch
	1. Resolution
	2. Color Depth

Pros & Cons 
- Photorealistic details of the photographs and the art works
- represent the wide range of color range and the gradients

- Raster images have a fixed resolution, leading to pixelation when resized

File types
- JPG
- PNG
- GIF
- TIFF

### <mark style="background: #FF5582A6;">Interlacing </mark>

*Interlacing is a description of how the picture is created, with an interlaced display the picture is created by scanning every other line, and on the next scan , scan every other opposite line.*

Pros & Cons

- Its allows for faster refresh rate

- May cause flickering or noticeable line movements in some situations

### Alternative  - Progressive Scanning


### <mark style="background: #FF5582A6;">Vector</mark>

*Vector used to display by geometrical privities [lines, Points, Curves, Polygons]*

Pros & Cons 
- Vector graphic can be moved or resized without loosing the quality or file size.

- Vectors are best suited for simple shapes, logos, illustrations

File types
- AI
- SVG
