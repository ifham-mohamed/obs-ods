# LAW - IT LAW

### IT Law in Sri Lanka

#### 1. **Introduction to IT Law**

- **Constitutional Background:** The Sri Lankan Constitution doesn’t specifically protect personal privacy, though there were attempts to include it in 1997 and 2000.
- **Early Laws:** Before 1997, there were few privacy laws. The Telecommunication Act of 1996 was one of the few laws addressing communication interception.
- **Need for Modern Laws:** With technology advancing quickly, new laws were needed to protect digital information and privacy. This led to laws like the Computer Crime Act and Electronic Transactions Act.

#### 2. **What is IT Law?**

- **Definition:** IT Law deals with handling digital information, including software protection, privacy, security, and online commerce.
- **Key Sources:**
    - **Local Laws:**
        - Computer Crime Act (2007)
        - Electronic Transactions Act (2006)
        - Data Protection Act
    - **Foreign Laws:**
        - UK’s Data Protection Act (1998)
        - India’s Information Technology Act (2000)

#### 3. **Major Related Laws**

- **Intellectual Property Act (2003):** Protects copyrights and patents to prevent piracy.
- **Computer Crime Act (2007):** Addresses cyber crimes like hacking and data theft.
- **Information and Communication Technology Act (2003):** Sets national policies for ICT development.
- **Payment Devices Frauds Act (2006):** Targets fraud related to payment devices like credit cards.
- **Electronic Transactions Act (2006):** Validates electronic contracts and communications.
- **Data Protection Act:** Ensures privacy and proper handling of personal data.

#### 4. **Recommendations for Improvement**

- **Software Licensing:** Enforce stricter rules against unauthorized software use.
- **Cybersecurity:** Improve measures to prevent hacking and secure systems.
- **Internet Access:** Expand access to both urban and rural areas.
- **Web Regulation:** Implement stricter rules for website creation to prevent cyber crimes.
- **ISP Control:** Better regulation and monitoring of internet service providers.

#### 5. **Sources of IT Law**

- **Common Law**: Legal principles developed through court decisions.
- **Legislation**: Laws enacted by the government.
- **Case Law**: Legal principles established through judicial decisions.
- **International Law**: Agreements and protocols between countries that affect IT law.

#### 6. **Conclusion**

- **Rapid Development:** IT is evolving quickly, so Sri Lanka needs to keep its laws updated to address new challenges.
- **Ongoing Improvement:** Continuous updates and improvements in IT laws are necessary to keep up with technological advancements and prevent cyber crimes.
