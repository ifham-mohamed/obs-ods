### <mark style="background: #FF5582A6;">Contrast</mark>
Contrast is the difference between various elements with in the design, that makes them standout from each other.

- draw out the most important elements of a design & add emphasis
- increase readability
- guide to viewer's eyes to the most important part in the design

**To strategically emphasize;** 
1. use size
2. color
3. type 
4. value. 

- Can use design elements that are in opposition to each other, like black and white, thick and thin, modern and traditional, etc.
- Consider using contrasting colors or pairing bold typefaces with delicate fonts to add variety .

It reinforce an idea or perception

**can repeat**

### <mark style="background: #FF5582A6;">Alignment</mark>
why?
- Stronger cohesiveness
- Connect spatially disconnected elements


- By ensuring various elements of design have a pleasing connection with each other. 
- Center, right, or left-aligned text is the most common. 
- Asymmetry and align text to other objects

### <mark style="background: #FF5582A6;">Proximity</mark>
- Items that are not related to each other should not be in close proximity. 
- The closeness or lack of closeness indicates the relationship. 
- Elements that are intellectually connected should be visually connected.

![[Graphic Systems - Design Principle - Proximity.png]]


### <mark style="background: #FF5582A6;">Balance</mark>
  
- Balance refers to the arrangement of shapes, illustrations on the page.
  
- Balance gives a design its form and stability.
  
- But it doesn’t mean elements need to be the same size, or that you must make symmetrical designs.
  
- The idea is to arrange elements within a layout, so they create a harmonious composition.
	1. Formal(Symmetrical) balance
	2. Informal(Asymmetrical) Balance
	3. Radial Balance and etc.

![[Graphic Systems - Design Principle - Balance.png]]


### <mark style="background: #FF5582A6;">Space</mark>

White space(or negative space) refers to area of a design that have no design elements.

- It doesn’t need to be white always.
	Can be aanempty or blank space surrounding all the other elements.

- Having blank spaces also important as the ones you’re filling with colors, text, and images.
	1. This space is important to make a design uncluttered, as well as for making various elements stand out.
	2. It provides visual breathing room for the eye.
	3. Add white space to make page less cramped, confusing, or overwhelming.

> [!Activity] Rule of Third
> The **Rule of Thirds** is a fundamental principle in both graph icdesign and photography. It involves dividing an image or layout into a 3x3 grid by using two equally spaced horizontal lines and two equally spaced vertical lines. This grid creates nine equal parts, resulting in four intersecting points. Here’s how it works:
> 1. **Composition Technique**: Imagine overlaying this grid on your design or photograph. You’ll have nine equal-shaped boxes. The rule of thirds guides you to strategically place key components (such as the main subject) along these lines or at the points where they intersect. This dynamic arrangement adds balance and visual interest to your composition.
> 2. **Balancing Elements**: By adhering to the rule of thirds, you can balance your subject with the surrounding white space, creating a harmonious and aesthetically pleasing result. [Whether you’re designing graphics, capturing photos, or creating UI layouts, this technique empowers you to create compelling visuals](https://www.interaction-design.org/literature/article/rule-of-thirds-examples)[1](https://www.interaction-design.org/literature/article/rule-of-thirds-examples)[2](https://www.creatopy.com/blog/what-is-rule-of-thirds/).
