# LAW - Condition

### Condition

A **Condition** in a contract is a fundamental term that is crucial to the contract's main purpose. If this term is not met, it is considered a serious breach. The affected party has the right to either cancel the contract or seek damages.

**Example:** Imagine you hire a contractor to build a house, and a key term is that the house must be completed by a certain date. If the contractor misses this deadline, it's a breach of condition. You could cancel the contract and claim damages for any loss.

### Warranty

A **Warranty** is a less critical term in the contract. It is important but does not go to the core of the agreement. If a warranty is breached, the affected party can only claim damages but cannot cancel the entire contract.

**Example:** If you buy a car and the dealer promises it will come with a full tank of gas, but only half a tank is provided, this is a breach of warranty. You can ask for compensation for the missing gas but can't cancel the entire purchase.

### In Summary

- **Condition**: Vital to the contract; breach allows for contract cancellation and damages.
- **Warranty**: Less crucial; breach only allows for claiming damages.
