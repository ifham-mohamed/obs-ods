# ECON -  Demand and Supply Together

### Recap of Key Concepts

- **Demand**: The quantity of a good or service that consumers are willing and able to purchase at various prices.
- **Supply**: The quantity of a good or service that producers are willing and able to sell at various prices.
- **Law of Demand**: As the price of a good decreases, the quantity demanded increases, and vice versa.
- **Law of Supply**: As the price of a good increases, the quantity supplied increases, and vice versa.
- **Variables Influencing Demand**: Factors such as income, tastes, prices of related goods, expectations, and number of buyers.
- **Variables Influencing Supply**: Factors such as production technology, costs of resources, prices of other goods, expectations, and number of sellers.

### Adjusting to Equilibrium

- **Surplus Adjustment**: Sellers lower prices to reduce excess inventory, attracting more buyers and reducing the quantity supplied until equilibrium is restored.
- **Shortage Adjustment**: Sellers raise prices due to increased demand or scarcity, reducing the quantity demanded and increasing the quantity supplied until equilibrium is restored

### Changes in Equilibrium

- **Example**: Growing public awareness about environmental issues increases demand for electric cars, shifting the demand curve to the right and increasing both equilibrium price and quantity
- **Example**: Advancements in battery technology reduce the cost of electric vehicles, shifting the supply curve to the right and decreasing equilibrium price while increasing equilibrium quantity



### How Prices Allocate Resources

1. **Prices as Signals**:
    - [Prices act as signals that guide economic decisions](https://edgeservices.bing.com/edgesvc/chat?udsframed=1&form=SHORUN&clientscopes=chat,noheader,udsedgeshop,channelstable,ntpquery,devtoolsapi,udsinwin11,udsdlpconsent,udsmrefresh,cspgrd,&shellsig=3622cedc03b18bc2132dc393e2b6a13249eddae2&setlang=en-US&darkschemeovr=1&udsps=0&udspp=0#sjevt%7CDiscover.Chat.SydneyClickPageCitation%7Cadpclick%7C1%7C5813f15a-4015-4b03-991e-a7b72d90dd47)[2](https://edgeservices.bing.com/edgesvc/chat?udsframed=1&form=SHORUN&clientscopes=chat,noheader,udsedgeshop,channelstable,ntpquery,devtoolsapi,udsinwin11,udsdlpconsent,udsmrefresh,cspgrd,&shellsig=3622cedc03b18bc2132dc393e2b6a13249eddae2&setlang=en-US&darkschemeovr=1&udsps=0&udspp=0#sjevt%7CDiscover.Chat.SydneyClickPageCitation%7Cadpclick%7C1%7C5813f15a-4015-4b03-991e-a7b72d90dd47). When prices change, they send messages to both buyers and sellers about the relative scarcity or abundance of goods and services.
2. **Balancing Supply and Demand**:
    - For every good in the economy, prices help ensure that supply and demand are balanced. When demand for a good increases, its price typically rises, signaling producers to supply more. Conversely, if demand decreases, prices fall, signaling producers to supply less.
3. **Equilibrium Price**:
    - [The equilibrium price, also known as the market-clearing price, is the price at which the quantity of goods buyers want to purchase equals the quantity sellers want to produce](https://edgeservices.bing.com/edgesvc/chat?udsframed=1&form=SHORUN&clientscopes=chat,noheader,udsedgeshop,channelstable,ntpquery,devtoolsapi,udsinwin11,udsdlpconsent,udsmrefresh,cspgrd,&shellsig=3622cedc03b18bc2132dc393e2b6a13249eddae2&setlang=en-US&darkschemeovr=1&udsps=0&udspp=0#sjevt%7CDiscover.Chat.SydneyClickPageCitation%7Cadpclick%7C2%7C5813f15a-4015-4b03-991e-a7b72d90dd47)[3](https://edgeservices.bing.com/edgesvc/chat?udsframed=1&form=SHORUN&clientscopes=chat,noheader,udsedgeshop,channelstable,ntpquery,devtoolsapi,udsinwin11,udsdlpconsent,udsmrefresh,cspgrd,&shellsig=3622cedc03b18bc2132dc393e2b6a13249eddae2&setlang=en-US&darkschemeovr=1&udsps=0&udspp=0#sjevt%7CDiscover.Chat.SydneyClickPageCitation%7Cadpclick%7C2%7C5813f15a-4015-4b03-991e-a7b72d90dd47). At this price, there is no surplus or shortage of goods.
4. **Decision Making**:
    - The equilibrium price determines how much of a good buyers choose to purchase and how much sellers choose to produce. Higher prices may encourage producers to increase supply, while lower prices may discourage production and reduce supply.

- **Prices Allocate Resources**: In market economies, prices guide economic decisions and allocate scarce resources, ensuring supply and demand are balanced.
