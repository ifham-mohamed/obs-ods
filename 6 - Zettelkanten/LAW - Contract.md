# LAW - Contract

### Definition and Explanation of a Contract

**What is a Contract?**

- **Simple Definition:** A contract is a legally binding agreement between two or more parties. It means that if one party doesn’t do what they agreed to, the other party can take legal action.

### Examples of Contracts

1. **Employment Contract:**
    - **Example:** When you get a job, you sign a contract that outlines your salary, job role, and working hours. If your employer doesn’t pay you, you can take legal action.
2. **Rental Agreement:**
    - **Example:** When you rent an apartment, you and the landlord sign a contract specifying the rent, lease duration, and other conditions. If either party breaks the terms, the other can enforce the contract legally.
3. **Purchase of Goods:**
    - **Example:** If you buy a car for Rs. 2,000,000 and the seller doesn’t deliver the car, you can seek legal recourse.

### Essential Elements of a Valid Contract

1. **Offer:**
    - **Definition:** A proposal by one party to another to enter into an agreement.
    - **Example:** “I will sell you my bike for Rs. 10,000.”
2. **Acceptance:**
    - **Definition:** When the other party agrees to the offer.
    - **Example:** “I agree to buy your bike for Rs. 10,000.”
3. **Consideration:**
    - **Definition:** What each party gives or receives in the contract.
    - **Example:** Rs. 10,000 from the buyer and the bike from the seller.
4. **Intention to Create Legal Relations:**
    - **Definition:** Both parties must intend for the agreement to be legally binding.
    - **Example:** A business contract is intended to be legally binding, unlike a casual promise to help a friend.
5. **Capacity to Contract:**
    - **Definition:** Parties must be legally capable of entering into a contract.
    - **Example:** They must be of legal age and mentally capable of understanding the terms.
6. **Legality of Purpose:**
    - **Definition:** The contract’s purpose must be legal.
    - **Example:** A contract to sell illegal drugs is not enforceable.
7. **Genuineness of Consent:**
    - **Definition:** Both parties must genuinely agree to the contract terms without coercion, fraud, or misrepresentation.
8. **No Public Policy Violation:**
    - **Definition:** The contract must not go against public policy.
    - **Example:** Agreements involving illegal activities are not enforceable.

### Examples in Sri Lanka

1. **Contract for Sale of Property:**
    - **Example:** If someone agrees to sell land for Rs. 5,000,000 and both parties sign a contract, it is enforceable in Sri Lankan courts. If the seller doesn’t transfer the property, the buyer can take legal action.
2. **Employment Contracts:**
    - **Example:** When a company hires an employee, they sign a contract outlining salary, duties, and duration. If the employer doesn’t follow the terms, the employee can take legal action.
3. **Service Contracts:**
    - **Example:** If a business agrees to provide a service, like construction work, for a specified amount, and fails to deliver, the client can enforce the contract.
