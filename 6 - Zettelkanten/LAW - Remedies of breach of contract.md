# LAW - Remedies of breach of contract

### Remedies for Breach of Contract

**1. Damages**

- **Definition:** Money paid to the injured party to cover their losses.
- **In Simple Terms:** If someone breaks a contract, the injured party can get money to cover their losses.
- **Types of Damages:**
    - **Ordinary Damages:** For natural losses from the breach.
    - **Special Damages:** For specific, proven losses.
    - **Exemplary Damages:** To punish the breaching party.
    - **Nominal Damages:** Small amount when there’s a breach but no real loss.
    - **Liquidated Damages:** Pre-agreed amounts for specific breaches.
    - **Unliquidated Damages:** Amounts decided by the court.

**2. Quantum Meruit**

- **Definition:** Payment for the value of work done.
- **In Simple Terms:** If you did work under a breached contract, you can get paid for the work you did.

**3. Specific Performance**

- **Definition:** Court order to fulfill the contract.
- **In Simple Terms:** Instead of money, the court can force the breaching party to do what they promised, like delivering a unique item.

**4. Injunction**

- **Definition:** Court order to stop someone from doing something.
- **In Simple Terms:** The court can stop someone from doing something harmful or against the contract.
    - **Permanent Injunction:** Long-term stop order.
    - **Interim Injunction:** Temporary stop order while the case is decided.

**5. Rescission**

- **Definition:** Canceling the contract.
- **In Simple Terms:** The injured party can cancel the contract and return to their original position, as if the contract never happened.

**6. Restitution in Integrum**

- **Definition:** Returning to the original state before the contract.
- **In Simple Terms:** The injured party is returned to their original state, including getting back anything given under the contract.
