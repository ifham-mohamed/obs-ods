# LAW - Judiciary System

*In the earlier lessons we discussed about Divisions of Law, Sources of Law and Legal Systems of Sri Lanka .The aim of this lesson is to give the basic knowledge about Courts of Sri Lanka(Judiciary system of Sri Lanka). It will enable you describe briefly the different types of courts of Law in Sri Lanka.*

### Sinhalese and Kandyan Courts

**During the Sinhalese Kings’ Time:**

- **King’s Role:** The king was the main authority for serious crimes and disputes involving priests and major offenses.
- **Types of Courts:**
    - **Gamsabawas (Village Councils):** Handled small cases like debts, petty thefts, and quarrels.
    - **Rata Sabhawas (District Councils):** Made up of delegates from each village in the district.
    - **Sake Ballanda (Court of Inquiry):** Similar to an English coroner’s court, it investigated causes of death.
    - **Government Officers:** Various officials had judicial roles, such as Vidanas, Koralas, and Village Headmen.
    - **Maha Naduwa (Great Court):** Consisted of high-ranking officials like Adigars and Dissawas.

### Portuguese and Dutch Courts

**Portuguese Period:**

- **Judicial Roles:** Judicial functions were carried out by officials like Koralas, Vidanes, and Dissawas.

**Dutch Period:**

- **Types of Courts:**
    - **Civile Raad (Civil Court):** Handled civil matters.
    - **Land Raad (Land Court):** Dealt with land issues.
    - **Raad Van Justitie (Court of Justice):** The main court for justice.

### Courts in Sri Lanka During the British Period

**Privy Council:**

- **Highest Court of Appeal:** The final court of appeal, located in Great Britain.

**Supreme Court:**

- **Highest Court in Sri Lanka:** The top court in Sri Lanka during the British period.

**Other Courts:**

1. **Courts of Criminal Appeal:** Handled appeals in criminal cases.
2. **District Courts:** Had original civil jurisdiction, meaning they handled civil cases first.
3. **Courts of Requests:** Also had original civil jurisdiction for smaller cases.
4. **Magistrate Courts:** Dealt with criminal cases.
5. **Rural Courts:** Also handled criminal cases, especially in rural areas.

In summary, the Privy Council was the highest appellate court, while the Supreme Court was the highest court within Sri Lanka. District Courts and Courts of Requests handled civil cases, and Magistrate and Rural Courts dealt with criminal cases.



### Modern Court System in Sri Lanka

**Constitutional Courts:**

- **Supreme Court of Sri Lanka:** The highest court in the country.
- **Court of Appeal of Sri Lanka:** Handles appeals from lower courts.
- **High Court of Sri Lanka:** Deals with serious criminal and civil cases.
- **Other Courts:** Created by Parliament as needed.

**Courts of First Instance (Judicature Act, No. 2 of 1978):**

- **High Court of Sri Lanka:** Handles both original and appellate jurisdiction.
- **District Courts:** Deal with civil cases.
- **Magistrate Courts:** Handle criminal cases.
- **Primary Courts:** Deal with minor civil and criminal matters.
- **Small Claims Courts:** Established by the 2022 amendment to handle small civil claims.

[These courts ensure the administration of justice across various levels and types of cases in Sri Lanka](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Judiciary_of_Sri_Lanka)[1](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Judiciary_of_Sri_Lanka)[2](https://factsanddetails.com/south-asia/Srilanka/Government_Justice_Military_Srilanka/entry-8018.html).

### Supreme Court of Sri Lanka

**Overview:**

- **Highest Court:** The Supreme Court is the top and final court in Sri Lanka.
- **Composition:** It consists of the Chief Justice and 16 other judges, appointed by the President with the recommendation of the Constitutional Council.

#### Jurisdiction (Powers and Functions)

1. **Constitutional Jurisdiction:**
    
    - **Article 120:** The Supreme Court can decide if any Bill (draft law) or part of it is inconsistent with the 1978 Constitution.
2. **Interpretation of the Constitution:**
    
    - **Article 125:** The Supreme Court has the exclusive power to interpret the Constitution. Its decisions are binding on Parliament.
3. **Fundamental Rights Jurisdiction:**
    
    - **Article 126:** The Supreme Court can hear cases about violations or potential violations of fundamental human rights or language rights declared by the Constitution.
4. **Final Appellate Jurisdiction:**
    
    - **Articles 127 and 128:** The Supreme Court is the final court for civil and criminal appeals. It can correct errors made by the Court of Appeal or any lower court. It can affirm, reverse, or change judgments and order new trials if necessary.
5. **Consultative Jurisdiction:**
    
    - **Article 129:** If a significant legal question arises, the President can ask the Supreme Court for its opinion.
6. **Breaches of Parliamentary Privilege:**
    
    - **Article 131:** The Supreme Court can punish anyone for breaching the privileges of Parliament.
7. **Election Petitions:**
    
    - **Article 130:** The Supreme Court can hear and decide on legal issues related to the election of the President and appeals from the Court of Appeal in election cases.
8. **Rule-Making Power:**
    
    - **Article 136:** The Supreme Court can make rules on various matters, such as procedures for hearing appeals, granting bail, and the admission, suspension, and removal of attorneys.
9. **Contempt of Court:**
    
    - The Supreme Court has the power to punish for contempt of court.

### Court of Appeal of Sri Lanka

**Composition:**

- **Judges:** The Court of Appeal consists of the President of the Court of Appeal and 19 other judges.

#### Jurisdiction (Powers and Functions)

1. **Inspect Records:**
    
    - The Court of Appeal can inspect and examine the records of any lower court, tribunal, or institution.
2. **Appellate Jurisdiction:**
    
    - **Civil and Criminal Appeals:** It can correct errors in fact and law made by any lower court or tribunal.
    - **Decisions:** The Court of Appeal can affirm, reverse, or change any judgment or order from a lower court. It can also order new trials or further hearings if needed.
3. **Issuing Writs:**
    
    - **Types of Writs:** The Court of Appeal can issue writs like Certiorari, Prohibition, Procedendo, Mandamus, and Quo Warranto.
    - **Habeas Corpus:** It can issue a writ to bring a person who is illegally detained before the court.
4. **Injunctions:**
    
    - **Preventing Harm:** The Court of Appeal can issue injunctions to prevent any irreparable harm before a case can be brought to a lower court.
5. **Election Petitions:**
    
    - **Parliamentary Elections:** The Court of Appeal can hear and decide on election petitions related to parliamentary elections.
6. **Contempt of Court:**
    
    - **Punishment:** The Court of Appeal has the power to punish for contempt of itself or any other court.

#### Courts of First Instance in Sri Lanka

According to the Judicature Act, No. 2 of 1978, the courts of first instance for the administration of justice are:

- **High Court of Sri Lanka**
- **District Courts**
- **Magistrate Courts**
- **Primary Courts**
- **Small Claims Courts** (added by the 2022 amendment)


### High Court of Sri Lanka

**Setup and Appointment:**

- **Judicial Zones:** High Courts are set up in different judicial zones across the country.
- **Judges:** Appointed by the President.

#### Jurisdiction (Powers and Functions)

1. **Original Criminal Jurisdiction:**
    
    - **Grave Offences:** The High Court handles serious crimes.
    - **Section 9 of the Judicature Act:** The High Court can hear and decide cases involving:
        - Crimes committed in Sri Lanka.
        - Crimes in Sri Lankan territorial waters.
        - Crimes in Sri Lankan airspace.
        - Crimes related to Sri Lankan ships or aircraft.
        - Crimes on the high seas (e.g., piracy).
2. **Trial Types:**
    
    - **Single Judge or Jury:** Trials can be held before a single judge or a jury.
    - **Trials-at-Bar:** For serious cases, a bench of three judges nominated by the Chief Justice.
3. **Provincial High Courts:**
    
    - **Established by the 13th Amendment (1987):** Each province has its own High Court.
    - **Appellate Jurisdiction:** Can review decisions from Magistrate Courts, Primary Courts, Labour Tribunals, etc.
    - **Habeas Corpus:** Can order the release of someone illegally detained.
    - **Writs:** Can issue orders like Certiorari, Prohibition, and Mandamus.
4. **Commercial High Court:**
    
    - **Specialized Court:** Handles cases under the Companies Act, commercial transactions over Rs. 20 million, and Intellectual Property Act cases.


### District Court

**Appointment:**

- **Judges:** All District Judges are appointed by the Judicial Service Commission.

#### Jurisdiction (Powers and Functions)

**Civil Matters:**

- **Unlimited Original Jurisdiction:** District Courts can handle all types of civil cases, including:
    - **Revenue:** Cases related to taxes and government income.
    - **Trusts:** Issues involving the management of trusts.
    - **Matrimonial:** Matters like divorce, annulment of marriage, judicial separation, custody of minor children, guardianship, and curator ship.
    - **Insolvency:** Cases involving bankruptcy.
    - **Testamentary:** Matters related to wills and inheritance.

**Specific Areas:**

- **Matrimonial Disputes:** Divorce, annulment, judicial separation, custody of children, guardianship, legitimacy and illegitimacy, and adoption.
- **Contracts:** Claims for breach of contracts and breach of promise of marriage.
- **Damages:** Applications for damages in cases like tort (delict).

In summary, District Courts handle a wide range of civil matters and have the authority to make decisions on these issues.

### Magistrates Court

**Appointment:**

- **Judges:** All Magistrates are appointed by the Judicial Service Commission.

#### Jurisdiction (Powers and Functions)

1. **Criminal Cases:**
    
    - **Penal Code and Other Laws:** Handles cases under the Penal Code and other laws like the Motor Traffic Act of 1951 and the Food Act of 1980.
    - **Summary Determination:** Can quickly decide cases without a full trial.
    - **Summons and Warrants:** Can issue summons for criminals and offenders, and search warrants.
    - **Inquiries:** Can investigate sudden or accidental deaths.
2. **Maintenance Applications:**
    
    - **Support:** Can handle applications for maintenance (financial support).

In summary, Magistrates Courts deal with various criminal cases, issue legal orders, and handle maintenance applications.



### Primary Courts

**Appointment:**

- **Judges:** All Primary Court judges are appointed by the Judicial Service Commission.

**Jurisdiction (Powers and Functions):**

- **Primary Courts Act of 1979:** Gives Primary Courts the power to handle petty crimes and land boundary disputes.
- **Criminal Jurisdiction:** Previously handled by Primary Courts, now transferred to Magistrate Courts.

### Small Claims Courts

- **Established by 2022 Amendment:** Handles claims below Rs. 1.5 million.

### Settlement of Disputes by Tribunals and Mediation Boards

**Mediation, Conciliation, and Arbitration:**

- **Arbitration:** Disputes are decided by a third party, governed by the Arbitration Act No. 11 of 1995.
- **Debt Conciliation Board:** Established under the Debt Conciliation Act to settle civil debt disputes.
- **Mediation Boards:** Established by the Mediation Boards Act No. 72 of 1988 to settle disputes related to property, debt, damage, or demands up to Rs. 500,000 (amended in 2016).

### Labor Tribunals

- **Established by Industrial Disputes (Amendment) Act of 1957:** Handles labor matters like termination of employment and gratuity issues.

### Quazi Courts

- **Muslim Marriage and Divorce Act No. 1951:** Provides for Quazi courts to handle claims for payments and maintenance for Muslims in Sri Lanka.
